Oily Skin:

If your skin looks shiny in appearance, especially on the forehead, nose, and chin, and it tends to produce excess oil on its own, you likely have oily skin. You may also notice frequent breakouts and enlarged pores.

Dry Skin:

If your skin often feels tight, rough, or flaky, especially after washing, and struggles to stay moisturized on its own, you likely have dry skin. It may feel uncomfortable or sensitive during weather changes.

If your skin feels balanced â€” not too oily or too dry â€” and looks smooth with barely visible pores, you likely have normal skin. It rarely experiences breakouts or sensitivity, and usually feels comfortable year-round.

Combination Skin:

If your skin feels oily in certain areas (like your T-zone: forehead, nose, chin) but normal or dry in other areas (like your cheeks), you likely have combination skin. It is the most common skin type and requires a mixed care routine.
